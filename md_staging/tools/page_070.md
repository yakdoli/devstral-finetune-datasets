## Docking CommandBars

### Overview
- This page describes the process of docking CommandBars to the target location in a Windows Forms application.
- Key steps include dragging the CommandBarController onto the form and adding a CommandBar through the design-time verb.
- Arranging buttons on the form is also covered.

### Content

![Form1 showing CommandBars docked to all the edges](images/Form1_CommandBars_Docked.jpg)
**Figure 17: Docking CommandBars**

#### Procedure

The following step-by-step procedure helps you to dock the CommandBar to the target location.

1. **Drag the CommandBarController onto the form and add a CommandBar through the design time verb.**

   - Drag the CommandBarController onto your form.
   - Right-click on the CommandBarController and select **Add CommandBar** from the context menu.

   ![Adding CommandBar Through Design Time Verb](images/Adding_CommandBar_Through_Design_Time_Verb.jpg)
   **Figure 18: Adding CommandBar Through Design Time Verb**

2. **Drag buttons onto the form and arrange the buttons as shown below.**

   - Drag buttons onto the form and arrange them as needed.
